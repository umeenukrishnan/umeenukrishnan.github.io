A researcher who loves to code.

I joined the Computational Mechanics Lab in January 2019, and since then, my work has primarily focused on the phase field method for fracture analysis and topology optimization. The phase field method is a powerful computational approach that allows for the simulation of crack initiation, propagation, and branching in materials without the need for complex tracking of crack surfaces.

In addition to fracture analysis, my research also extends to topology optimization, which is a computational technique used to design optimal material distributions within a given design space. By integrating the phase field method with topology optimization, I aim to develop innovative designs that not only meet performance criteria but also have enhanced durability and resistance to fracture.

My work has involved extensive use of open-source software such as FEniCS for finite element analysis, which has greatly facilitated the implementation and testing of our models.

If you want to discuss high performance computing with the finite element method, I would happily chat with you. To schedule an appointment, please book a time on my calendar.
